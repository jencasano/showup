Side B · full mockups · 8b vs 8d

showup. / Option 8, two ways

Full reference-quality mockups for the two Option 8 sidebar candidates you're torn between. Same palette, same tracker, same drifting motifs. Only the sidebar bg changes. Scroll, feel, pick.

8b
Near-black ink sidebar
architectural · sharp object on maroon paper
The pitch. Sidebar is a deep cool-black at 85% opacity with blur, so it reads as a distinct object sitting in front of the maroon paper. More contrast, more separation between chrome and content. The active ink-pill blends naturally into the dark sidebar, which makes it feel like part of a single dark zone rather than a leap. Most "app-like" of the two. Best if you want the content to feel spotlighted.

8d
Warm leather sidebar
cozy · warmer than paper · harmony-forward
The pitch. Sidebar is a warmer brown than the maroon paper, at 78% opacity with soft blur. The whole page reads as warm-on-warm, with the sidebar the "aged leather" and the content the "polished leather." Least contrasty of the pair. Feels most like showup.'s warmth continuing into dark mode, not interrupting it. Best for long reading and journaling sessions. Softest personality.

NOTES
Reading the pair
two different showup.s
8b makes the sidebar a distinct object. The near-black creates sharp separation from the maroon paper, which spotlights the content. If you love dark-mode apps that feel focused and architectural (Linear, Raycast, etc.), this one speaks that language.

8d keeps the warmth uninterrupted. Everything is leather, everything is aged paper. Softest transition from Side A. If you want dark mode to feel like "showup. in the evening" rather than "a different showup.," this one wins.

Drifting motifs (stars, dots, squiggles) in both. Check if they read better against the near-black of 8b or the warmer brown of 8d. My instinct is they glow more distinctively on 8d.

Pick and we lock Side B. 🎧
