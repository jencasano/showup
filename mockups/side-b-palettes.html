Four emotional targets for Side B, presented as palette studies with a mini preview of how each would feel applied to the locked sidebar + stripe + tracker. The red in each option is tuned to the background it sits on, not the same #C3342B from Side A. A fifth slot is reserved for the VSCode theme reference you're about to share.

OPTION 1
Aged tobacco / leather bar
deep warm browns · amber ghost · masculine, contemplative
The feeling. Sitting in a cognac bar with low lighting. Not black, but a deep espresso brown with a ghost of amber warmth in the shadows. Text is a soft warm cream with a slightly amber tint, like old paper under a bar lamp. Most distinctively "Mixtape" of the four. Risk: can feel a touch masculine if the red is too aggressive.

OPTION 2
Velvet record sleeve (oxblood)
deep burgundy-black · cinematic · slightly dramatic
The feeling. The inside of a vintage vinyl sleeve. A deep oxblood that's almost burgundy-black, warmer and more romantic than Option 1. Most cinematic of the four. OPTION 3

OPTION 4
Midnight blue, done right
cold where the others are warm · crisp · editorial
The feeling. Inside the Met at closing time. Not Claude blue. A properly cold, inky navy that leans editorial instead of SaaS. Biggest risk: any dark-blue dark mode risks reading as a generic "dark theme," which is precisely what Mixtape is trying to escape. Included for completeness. My instinct is we don't pick this unless it wins on its own merit when placed next to the others.
