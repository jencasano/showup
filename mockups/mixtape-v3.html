Mixtape · v3 · sidebar treatments

showup. / three sidebar attempts at not being a SaaS app

Same palette. Same texture (Level 3). Same Manrope body. Same tracker. Only the left column changes. Scroll through, feel the difference, pick the one that reads most like a thing someone made, not a thing a system generated.

A
Blended sidebar
no background color, hairline edge, nav items in display serif
What this is trying to do. Remove the "panel" feeling entirely. The sidebar is just the left margin of the page, like the binding margin of a book. Nav items are set in Fraunces (the display serif) at a comfortable reading size, separated by dashed underline on hover. Active item is italic with a small red dot out in the margin. A tiny hand-drawn-feel motif sits above the logo to signal "a person made this."

B
Soft textured panel
slightly darker paper, same grain, subtle motif at the top
What this is trying to do. Keep the sidebar as a distinct area so eye anchor is easy, but make it feel like a different paper stock, not a UI panel. Background is paper-deep (a touch darker than the main cream). The same grain shows through. Nav items are compact rows with a small bullet that lights up red when active. Small hand-drawn motif in red above the logo. This is the middle-ground: recognizable as a sidebar, but with warmth and texture instead of flat chrome.

C
Dark panel, typographic nav
deep ink sidebar, large italic serif nav, one tiny yellow motif
What this is trying to do. Keep the sidebar as a distinct visual moment, but make that moment feel editorial, not system. The panel is deep ink. Nav items are set in large Fraunces at reading size, most are quiet and faded; active item is paper-white italic with a small red dot. A single hand-drawn motif in warm yellow sits at the very top. The whole sidebar reads like the table of contents page of a well-made book, not a nav drawer.

What to react to
one sidebar wins, or we Frankenstein a v4
A: Most editorial, riskiest, most unlike any SaaS app. Might feel too understated on mobile. My vote if you want to be brave.

B: Middle ground. Panel is still there but it reads as a different paper stock, not UI chrome. Safest way to keep a sidebar while losing the SaaS feeling.

C: Strongest visual anchor. The dark panel gives the page a backbone. Nav items read like a table of contents. This is the most "object-like" option, the most premium.

On the motif: each sidebar has a different little hand-drawn mark at the top (A: squiggle-dot-star, B: wave-and-dot in red, C: continuous wave in yellow). These are placeholders. Once we pick a sidebar we pick the real motif, and it becomes a small brand element we use elsewhere.

Also feel free to say: none of these, try again.
