03
Nav direction B · Mixtape Cover
tall header as album sleeve, tabs as chapters rising from the page
The header is a deep ink slab with the logo big and the tagline beside it in italic serif. Tabs don't sit under a neutral bar; they're chapters rising into the page, with the active one fully inverted to paper color so it reads like it's been opened to that spot. Subtle diagonal stripe on the right is the only texture, a nod to a cassette label without being literal.

Typography · three candidates
Sora stays as the logo · we pick the display face
The logo font stays (Sora, 800). We're picking the display serif that sets month headings, zone titles, and emotional copy. Body stays Inter (quiet, legible). Caveat stays for diary handwriting. Each card shows the same layout, only the display font changes. Pick the one that feels most like a Side A.
A · Fraunces
warm / classic
April 2026
day 18 of 30 · side A
Fraunces is what showup. already uses. Warm, slightly curvy, deeply competent. The italic has character. Feels lived-in. Feels like a well-made journal.
Wrote in the morning before anyone was awake. Good coffee. Kept it to half a page.

editorial / striking

Instrument Serif is tall and confident. More magazine-editorial than journal. Higher drama. The italic has sharper personality. Risk: might feel too fashion-editorial for a daily tracker.

05
What to react to
this is a starting point, not a proposal for implementation
Palette: feel right? Too warm? Too subdued? The cream leans a touch yellow, not gray. If you want cooler cream, say so.

Nav A vs B: A is quieter and scalable, B is more distinctive and frames the whole app. Not mutually exclusive forever, but we pick one for launch.

Typography: my gut says A (Fraunces) with Archivo reserved for callouts, tags, and Shop pricing. But react honestly.

Texture: look closely at the paper background. That's a noise grain. Every surface has a quiet grain underneath. That's the "subtle grain only where it matters" choice you picked earlier. Nothing louder than this.
